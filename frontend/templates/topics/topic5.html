🔹 Phishing Prevention

Prevention is better than cure. Implement these technical controls and user training programs.

Technical Controls:
1. SPF/DKIM/DMARC: Configure strict DMARC rejection (p=reject) to prevent domain spoofing
2. Email Gateway: Deploy Proofpoint, Mimecast, or Microsoft Defender for Office 365 — scans all inbound/outbound email
3. URL Sandboxing: Rewrite all URLs in emails, check reputation in real-time before allowing clicks
4. Attachment Sandboxing: Detonate all Office documents, PDFs, and archives in a sandbox before delivery
5. DMARC Reporting: Monitor DMARC aggregate reports to detect spoofing attempts

User Training Program:
- Monthly simulated phishing: Send fake phishing emails to employees, track who clicks
- Reporting button: One-click Outlook plugin to report suspicious emails
- Training modules: Annual mandatory security awareness training
- Reward program: Gift cards for employees who report real phishing emails

Metrics — Before vs After Training Program:
Before: 35% click rate → After 6 months: 8% click rate → After 12 months: 3% click rate
Report rate: 2% → 45% of suspicious emails reported before incident occurs

🔹 Real-Phish Investigations

Investigate real-world phishing campaigns to understand attacker infrastructure and identify compromised accounts.

Real Phishing Investigation Case:
Situation: 15 employees report receiving a fake DocuSign email. 3 employees entered their credentials.

Investigation:
1. Email analysis: Headers show all emails from same infrastructure (IP 45.33.32.0/24), same phishing kit (same HTML structure, same exfiltration endpoint)
2. Domain analysis: docusign-verify[.]com registered 3 days ago, hosted on bulletproof hosting in Russia
3. Phishing kit analysis: Downloaded from the compromised server. Kit exfiltrates credentials to attacker@protonmail.com and stores them in /admin/logs.txt
4. Impact assessment: 3 users entered credentials. Check O365 sign-in logs: attacker accessed 2 mailboxes from IP 185.220.101.x at 03:00 UTC. Attacker searched for "password", "bank", "wire" in emails. No wire transfers occurred (financial team caught it).
5. Remediation: Reset 3 user passwords, revoke O365 sessions, enable MFA, block attacker IPs and domain at firewall, take down phishing site via hosting provider abuse contact, report to law enforcement
6. Lessons learned: Users didn't recognize "docusign-verify" as fake. Add domain similarity checks to email gateway. Increase phishing simulation frequency from monthly to bi-weekly.
