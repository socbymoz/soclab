Step 5: Incident Response

Execute containment and eradication actions to stop the attack and remove the threat
What Is Incident Response and What Actions Are Taken?

Incident Response (IR) is the organized approach to addressing and managing a security incident. The goal is to stop the attack, prevent further damage, and remove the attacker's access.

Following the NIST SP 800-61 framework, IR has four phases:

PhaseWhat It DoesExamples
1. ContainmentStop the attack from spreadingIsolate systems, block IPs, disable accounts
2. EradicationRemove the attacker's presenceDelete malware, remove persistence, kill processes
3. RecoveryRestore normal operations safelyRestore from backup, reimage systems
4. Post-IncidentLearn and improveReport, lessons learned, improve controls
How to Prioritize Response Actions

When responding to an incident, use this priority framework:

  • Stop the bleeding first — isolate affected systems, block attacker IPs at the firewall. Nothing else matters if the attacker is still active.
  • Preserve evidence — take disk images and memory captures before deleting anything. You may need evidence for legal action.
  • Remove access — disable compromised accounts, revoke sessions, reset passwords.
  • Remove the threat — delete malware, remove persistence mechanisms, patch vulnerabilities.
Dangerous Indicators That Trigger Response
  • CRITICAL Active C2 beaconing — attacker is currently communicating with compromised systems
  • CRITICAL Data exfiltration — sensitive data is being uploaded to attacker servers
  • CRITICAL Ransomware execution — files are being encrypted
  • HIGH New admin accounts created — privilege escalation in progress
  • HIGH Registry persistence — attacker has established a foothold
  • HIGH Audit log clearing — attacker covering tracks
