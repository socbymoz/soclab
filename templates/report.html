Why Is Incident Reporting Important?

An incident report is a formal document that summarizes the entire incident lifecycle. It serves multiple audiences:

Management
Needs executive summary, business impact, financial cost, and regulatory implications
SOC Team
Needs technical details — IOCs, timeline, attack vector, TTPs used, what worked and what didn't
Legal & Compliance
Needs evidence chain of custody, data breach notification details, regulatory compliance proof
What Makes a Good Incident Report?
  • Executive Summary — one page that tells the whole story: what happened, what was affected, what was done
  • Timeline — chronological list of key events from initial access to containment
  • Findings — root cause analysis, attack vector, IOCs discovered, systems affected
  • Actions Taken — containment, eradication, and recovery steps executed
  • Evidence — log excerpts, screenshots, file hashes, network captures
  • Recommendations — security improvements to prevent recurrence
Tip: Write the report as if the attacker will read it. Assume every detail will be scrutinized in court or by regulators. Precision matters — use exact times, exact commands, exact IP addresses.
