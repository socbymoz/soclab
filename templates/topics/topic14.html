Module 14: SOC Level 1 Capstone Challenges
Sub-Domains / Rooms
  • Tempest — Full incident investigation: correlate PCAP, SIEM data, and email artifacts to trace the attack from initial access to exfiltration.
  • Boogeyman 1 — Investigate a multi-stage attack involving phishing, malware, and lateral movement across the network.
  • Boogeyman 2 — Deep-dive forensics: analyze memory dumps, registry hives, and timeline reconstruction.
  • Boogeyman 3 — Final phase: identify the threat actor, attribute the attack, and recommend remediation measures.
  • Hidden Hooks — Investigate a hidden persistence mechanism and trace the attacker's post-exploitation activities.
  • Open Door — Analyze an open-source intelligence (OSINT) investigation tied to a data breach scenario.
  • Upload and Conquer — Investigate a web application breach involving file upload abuse and web shell deployment.
  • BlackCat — Investigate a BlackCat/ALPHV ransomware incident: identify initial access, propagation, and encryption scope.
Example: In the "Tempest" challenge, the analyst receives three artifacts: a PCAP file, SIEM logs, and a phishing email. The email shows the initial access (phishing link), the PCAP shows C2 beaconing and data exfiltration (2.3GB to external IP), and the SIEM logs show lateral movement (pass-the-hash to 12 servers). The analyst builds a complete timeline, identifies the root cause (employee clicked phishing link), determines the scope (12 servers compromised, customer data exfiltrated), and writes a report with containment and recovery recommendations.
Key Skills Learned
  • Multi-source evidence correlation
  • Full attack chain reconstruction
  • Incident timeline creation
  • Remediation and reporting
