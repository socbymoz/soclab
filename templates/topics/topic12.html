🔹 Intro to Cyber Threat Intel

CTI is evidence-based knowledge about threats to inform defensive decisions. There are four types of CTI:

The Four Types of Threat Intelligence:

1. Strategic CTI (Executive Level): High-level reports for CISOs and board members
Example: "Ransomware-as-a-Service groups increased 45% this year. LockBit is the most active, targeting manufacturing and healthcare. Recommend: increase offline backup frequency, implement network segmentation, deploy EDR on all endpoints."

2. Tactical CTI (SOC Manager Level): TTPs, tools, and techniques of specific adversaries
Example: "APT29 (Cozy Bear) uses T1566.001 (Spear-phishing), T1059.003 (PowerShell), T1003.001 (LSASS dumping). They're actively targeting vaccine researchers. Deploy detections for these specific techniques."

3. Operational CTI (SOC Analyst Level): Specific upcoming attacks, campaigns, and threat actor activity
Example: "A new phishing campaign using fake DocuSign emails is targeting your industry. Subject: 'Document Ready for Signature'. C2: docusign-verify[.]com (185.130.5.201). Update your email gateway rules to block."

4. Technical CTI (Analysis Level): IOCs — hashes, IPs, domains, YARA rules
Example: "Block these IOCs: SHA256 3f5b8c2a..., IP 45.33.32.156, domain evil-update.com"

The CTI Lifecycle:
Direction (what to prioritize) → Collection (gather data) → Processing (analyze, extract IOCs) → Analysis (produce intelligence) → Dissemination (share with stakeholders) → Feedback (improve future collection)

Threat Intel Integration with SOC
How to Operationalize Threat Intel:
1. Ingest feeds: Subscribe to threat intel feeds relevant to your industry (e.g., healthcare, finance)
2. Automate enrichment: Configure SOAR to automatically check IPs/domains/hashes against threat intel when alerts fire
3. Block known bad: Firewall rules for known malicious IP ranges, DNS sinkhole for known malicious domains
4. Detection rules: Create SIEM rules that match against known TTPs of threat actors targeting your industry
5. Proactive hunting: Use threat intel to proactively search for signs of specific threat actors in your environment
6. Sharing: Share your own IOCs with trusted partners via MISP or FS-ISAC
