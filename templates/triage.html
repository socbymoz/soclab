What Is Triage and Why Is It Critical?

Definition: What Is Triage?

Triage is the process of quickly evaluating alerts to determine which are real threats (True Positives) and which are false alarms. Borrowed from emergency medicine — treat the most critical first.

TP = Real Threat FP = False Alarm BP = Benign
Filter. Prioritize. Escalate.
Real Triage Example
Triage queue — alerts needing classification:

Result: 5 alerts → 5 True Positives → Escalate to Investigation
Key insight: Not every alert is a real threat. Triage separates the signal from the noise so investigators don't waste time on false positives.
