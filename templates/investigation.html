Step 4: Incident Investigation

Deep dive — correlate evidence across log sources to understand the full attack chain
What Happens During Investigation?

After triage identifies a True Positive alert, the investigation phase begins. This is where you move from "something suspicious happened" to understanding exactly what happened, how, and what the impact is.

Key Investigation Activities
1. Timeline Reconstruction Order events chronologically across all log sources. The timeline tells the story — initial access → lateral movement → privilege escalation → C2 → exfiltration.
2. IOC Identification Extract Indicators of Compromise: IP addresses, domains, file hashes, registry paths, process names. These IOCs are used to search for the attacker across other systems.
3. Artifact Analysis Examine the actual files, scripts, and network traffic involved. What does the malware do? What data was accessed?
4. Scope Determination How many systems are affected? What data or systems are at risk? Is the attacker still active?
