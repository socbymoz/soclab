What Is an Alert and How Is It Different From a Log?

A log is a raw record of an event. An alert is a log that matches a known pattern of malicious or suspicious activity. Think of it this way:

1,000

Raw logs per minute

10

Actual alerts generated

In a SOC, automated rules or SIEM correlation logic filter the noise and generate alerts for events that match attack signatures, behavioral anomalies, or policy violations.

Common Alert Triggers
  • Signature-based: Known attack patterns like SQL injection (1=1, UNION SELECT), path traversal (../)
  • Anomaly-based: Behavior outside normal baseline — login at 3 AM, outbound connection to unknown IP
  • Threshold-based: 10+ failed logins in 1 minute = brute force alert
  • Correlation: Failed SSH login + new admin account + C2 traffic = multi-stage attack
